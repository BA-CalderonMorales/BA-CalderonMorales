# Philosophy

How I think about building software.

## Core Beliefs

**Constraints force clarity.** My entire development workflow runs in Termux on Android. When you have limited screen space and compute, you stop tolerating bloat.

**Documentation is part of the product.** Not an afterthought. I write docs-as-code into the workflow so onboarding and maintenance do not drift apart.

**Systems over scripts.** I build infrastructure that stays coherent when complexity piles up. A script solves today's problem. A system solves tomorrow's too.

**Operational clarity under pressure.** Five years as a Legal Chief and Court Reporter in the USMC taught me that when stakes are high, ambiguity kills. Clear process, clear accountability.

## How I Work

**Calm pipelines.** Predictable GitHub Actions and release routines over clever systems that are hard to debug at 2 AM. Migrated 20+ teams from legacy Jenkins to maintainable automation.

**Security as baseline.** Fortify, ScanCentral, dependency hygiene, and access control are part of the build, not cleanup work.

**Coach through clarity.** I mentor by pairing, documenting tradeoffs, and keeping operational steps visible. The AGENTS.md files in my repos exist so anyone (human or AI) can understand context without me repeating myself.

**AI-augmented, not AI-dependent.** Use agents to reduce friction, not to avoid thinking. The terminal jarvis projects in this profile are built on that principle.

## Selected Principles

- Small, testable changes. Large refactors fail in production.
- Version everything. If it is not in git, it does not exist.
- Mobile-ready environments. Constraints force clarity.
- Agentic workflows that actually ship.

## Background

Currently senior engineer at Fiserv, leading DevOps standardization across 300+ repositories and working with the GitHub Copilot team on agentic workflows.

Before this: Leidos, SAIC, and five years as a Legal Chief and Court Reporter in the USMC.
